Tumor Feature Analysis

This tool uses 30 tumor cell nucleus measurements computed from digitized FNA images to predict if a tumor is benign or malignant.

Understanding Feature Categories

Each of the 10 cell nucleus features is measured in three ways:

  • Mean: Average value across all cells
  • SE: Standard Error (measurement variability)
  • Worst: Mean of the 3 largest/worst values
Feature Definitions
Radius Mean distance from center to perimeter
6.9 - 28.1

Radius measures the size of the cell nucleus. Larger radius values often indicate malignancy as cancer cells tend to be larger than normal cells.

Mean example: 14.1 | SE example: 0.4 | Worst example: 16.8
Texture Standard deviation of gray-scale values
9.7 - 39.3

Texture measures the variation in pixel intensity. Higher texture values indicate more irregular cell surfaces, which can be a sign of malignancy.

Mean example: 19.3 | SE example: 1.2 | Worst example: 25.5
Perimeter Total boundary length of the nucleus
43.8 - 188.5

Perimeter is the total distance around the cell nucleus boundary. Larger perimeters correlate with larger, potentially malignant cells.

Mean example: 91.9 | SE example: 2.9 | Worst example: 107.3
Area Total area of the cell nucleus
143 - 2501

Area represents the size of the nucleus in square units. Malignant tumors typically have larger nuclear areas due to uncontrolled cell growth.

Mean example: 654.9 | SE example: 40.3 | Worst example: 880.6
Smoothness Local variation in radius lengths
0.05 - 0.16

Smoothness measures how uniform the cell boundary is. Lower values indicate smoother, more regular boundaries typical of benign cells.

Mean example: 0.096 | SE example: 0.007 | Worst example: 0.132
Compactness Perimeter^2 / Area - 1.0
0.02 - 0.35

Compactness measures how circular the nucleus is. Higher values indicate more irregular shapes, which can suggest malignancy.

Mean example: 0.104 | SE example: 0.025 | Worst example: 0.254
Concavity Severity of concave portions
0.0 - 0.43

Concavity measures the presence and depth of indentations in the cell boundary. Higher concavity often indicates malignant characteristics.

Mean example: 0.089 | SE example: 0.032 | Worst example: 0.272
Concave Points Number of concave portions
0.0 - 0.20

Counts the number of indentations in the nucleus boundary. More concave points can indicate irregular cell boundaries seen in malignant tumors.

Mean example: 0.049 | SE example: 0.015 | Worst example: 0.114
Symmetry Symmetry of the cell nucleus
0.11 - 0.30

Measures how symmetric the cell nucleus is. Malignant cells often show asymmetry due to irregular growth patterns.

Mean example: 0.181 | SE example: 0.021 | Worst example: 0.290
Fractal Dimension Coastline approximation - 1
0.05 - 0.10

Fractal dimension measures the complexity of the cell boundary. Higher values indicate more irregular, complex boundaries associated with malignancy.

Mean example: 0.063 | SE example: 0.004 | Worst example: 0.084
